About Me

Current position: PhD student at Universitat of Barcelona.
On the right a relaxed description... if you want a professional description, read the cards below.


My name is Josep Ferré I am a PhD student at the University of Barcelona under the supervision of Prof. Aurora Hernandez-Machado.

Besides that, I am also a skydiver (17 freefalls) and scuba diver (40+ dives).

However, I have been fascinated since I got to know the results of Artificial Intelligence in the context of Deep Learning and Big Data. It is really fascinating seeing that we can train machines to understand images and even make them tell you what they are seeing. What deep learning is capable of achieving goes beyond my imagination, and that is why I love exploring different use cases.

I have grown in a small town with less than 300 people censed, surrounded by vineyards where my family cultivated grapes to produce wine. We used to have a coop full of chickens, geese, ducks, pigeons and even peacocks. We also had rabbits, but I don't remember that anymore.

Despite my great childhood (or thanks to it), I am passionate about mechanisms, know-how, technology and investments. The reason why I took the theoretical branch of physics and started investing as soon as I could.

But it wasn't until recently that I discovered that I loved Artificial Intelligence and Electronics in my first start-up job. I even bought a Raspberry PI and sensors to start exploring the field.

About Me as a Researcher

Industrial PhD student, Computational Condensed Matter Physics

Industrial PhD student at Rheo Diagnostics S.L. and Universitat of Barcelona.

My research is shifting towards Scientific Machine Learning: the derivation of system models and integration with artificial intelligence techniques like neural networks. Using scientific (differential equation) and data-driven models, the simulators can be improved to better approximate modelled systems.

I started with phase field models while completing my Master's and PhD at the University of Barcelona, Spain. My research with my advisor, Dra. Aurora Hernandez-Machado focused on the methods for simulating cell advancement and proliferation (angiogenesis); biological models using phase field models. However, together we did some blood state characterisation work by using microfluidic systems with machine learning classification algorithms.

About Me as a Team Lead & Product Manager

Private investment consultant for investors in the biotech sector
